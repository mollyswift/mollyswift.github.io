Carbon Sequestration- Beara Peninsula, Ireland

My region of interest for this map is the Beara Peninsula in Ireland. This is atually where I am from, I was eager to learn more about the area and this way I could. The peninsula has cool-temperate oceanic climate with high precipitation levels year round. The area has a very rugged and hilly terrain and being a peninsula has a large coastline.

This map measure a regions ability to sequester carbon by looking into gross primary production Above ground carbon density, forest cover, these all then factor into the Carbon Sequestration Potential Index. As you can see in the map above there is high growth primary production throughout my entire region of interest, this is a positive indicator, as a high GPP means that plants are absorbing more carbon dioxide from the atmosphere.

To create this map I selected my region on Geojason and dowloaded it as a zip file. I then imported this into Google Earth Engine. I found the coordinates for the region and downloaded the corresponding ESA data and imported it into GEE. Then I used a java script code to create this map.
